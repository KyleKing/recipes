Hidden Veggie Pasta Sauce

Based on The Balanced Nutritionist (wayback) and Yummy Toddler Food (wayback)

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium zucchini, chopped (about 1.5 cups)
  • 1 large carrot, peeled and chopped (about 1 cup)
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 cup vegetable or chicken broth
  • Optional: 1 cup fresh spinach or kale

Recipe

  1. Heat olive oil in large pot over medium heat. Add onion and cook until softened, 5-7 minutes
  2. Add garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute
  3. Add zucchini, carrot, cauliflower, and bell pepper. Cook, stirring occasionally, until vegetables begin to soften, 8-10 minutes
  4. Add crushed tomatoes, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine
  5. Add broth and bring to boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 20-25 minutes until all vegetables are very tender
  6. If using spinach or kale, add during last 5 minutes of cooking
  7. Remove from heat and let cool slightly, about 5 minutes
  8. Using immersion blender, blend sauce until completely smooth. Or transfer in batches to regular blender and blend until smooth
  9. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Add pinch of sugar if sauce is too acidic
  10. Serve over pasta with parmesan cheese if desired

Notes

  • Freezer-friendly: Freeze in portions (2-cup containers or ice cube trays) for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in fridge or microwave from frozen
  • Vegetable variations: Swap or add butternut squash, sweet potato, mushrooms, or celery. Keep total vegetable amount the same (about 5 cups chopped)
  • Protein additions: Stir in cooked ground turkey, chicken, or plant-based crumbles after blending
  • Texture preference: For chunkier sauce, blend only half or pulse briefly instead of fully smooth
  • Flavor boosters: Add 1/4 tsp red pepper flakes for adults, fresh basil at end, or splash of balsamic vinegar
  • Storage: Refrigerate in airtight container up to 5 days
  • Serving ideas: Use with any pasta shape, in lasagna, as pizza sauce, or in baked ziti
  • Kid involvement: Let kids help add ingredients to pot (supervised) and press blender button
  • Make ahead: Sauce actually tastes better next day as flavors meld
  • Batch cooking: Double or triple recipe for meal prep
  • See Kids Meal Adaptations for general guidance
