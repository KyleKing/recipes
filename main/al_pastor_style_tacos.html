Al Pastor-Style Tacos

Based on https://www.seriouseats.com/tacos-al-pastor-recipe (wayback) and https://www.thekitchn.com/al-pastor-tacos-recipe-23675063 (wayback unavailable) and https://rainbowplantlife.com/vegan-al-pastor-tacos/ (wayback unavailable)

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 3 dried guajillo chiles (or 2 Tbsp chili powder if unavailable)
  • 2 dried ancho chiles (or substitute more guajillo)
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice (from canned pineapple or fresh)
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar or apple cider vinegar
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried oregano (Mexican oregano preferred)
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 Tbsp achiote paste (or 1 tsp paprika + 1/2 tsp turmeric for color)

Protein (choose one)

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder, thinly sliced (traditional)
  • 2 lbs boneless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
  • 2 blocks (14-16 oz each) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed (vegan)
  • 2 cans (15 oz each) pinto or black beans, drained (bean-based option)

For Cooking and Serving

  • 2 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cup fresh pineapple, diced (or canned, drained)
  • 1/2 medium white onion, finely diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges
  • Corn tortillas (small, street taco size)
  • Optional: salsa, hot sauce, radishes, avocado

Recipe

Prepare the Marinade

  1. If using dried chiles: Remove stems and seeds, tear into pieces, and toast in a dry skillet over medium heat for 1-2 minutes until fragrant (do not burn). Soak in hot water for 15 minutes until softened
  2. Add softened chiles (or chili powder), pineapple juice, vinegar, garlic, cumin, oregano, cinnamon, cloves, salt, pepper, and achiote paste to a blender. Blend until smooth

Marinate the Protein

  1. For pork or chicken: Place sliced meat in a bowl or zip-top bag, pour marinade over, and toss to coat. Marinate for at least 2 hours or overnight in refrigerator
  2. For tofu: Press tofu to remove excess moisture, cut into cubes, and marinate for at least 1 hour (or up to 8 hours)
  3. For beans: Toss beans with marinade and let sit for 30 minutes to 2 hours

Cook

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet or griddle over medium-high heat
  2. For pork/chicken: Cook marinated meat in batches until edges are charred and meat is cooked through (6-8 minutes per batch). Remove and let rest, then chop into smaller pieces
  3. For tofu: Cook cubes until edges are crispy and caramelized (8-10 minutes), turning occasionally
  4. For beans: Cook beans with marinade over medium heat until liquid reduces and beans are slightly crispy on edges (8-10 minutes)
  5. In the same pan, add diced pineapple and cook until caramelized and slightly charred (3-4 minutes)

Assemble

  1. Warm tortillas on a griddle or directly over a gas flame
  2. Fill tortillas with cooked protein and caramelized pineapple
  3. Top with diced onion, fresh cilantro, and a squeeze of lime
  4. Serve with additional lime wedges and optional toppings

Notes

  • Traditional al pastor uses pork shoulder slow-cooked on a vertical spit (trompo) with pineapple on top, but this home version achieves similar flavors
  • Achiote paste adds authentic red color and earthy flavor; find it in Mexican grocery stores or online
  • For spicier tacos, add 1-2 chipotle peppers in adobo to the marinade
  • Dried guajillo and ancho chiles provide the best flavor, but chili powder works as a substitute
  • For extra char flavor, finish protein under the broiler for 2-3 minutes
  • Leftover protein stores well in refrigerator for 3-4 days and reheats nicely
