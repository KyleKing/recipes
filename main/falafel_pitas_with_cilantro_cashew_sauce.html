Falafel Pitas With Cilantro-Cashew Sauce

Based on https://www.washingtonpost.com/recipes/… (wayback unavailable) (wayback unavailable)

Personal rating: 3 / 5

Ingredients

For The Sauce

See note at bottom for alternative yogurt-based sauce instead of cashew-based

  • 1 cup (5 oz) raw, unsalted cashews, soaked (10-12 hours!)
  • 1/2 bunch fresh cilantro leaves (1 cup packed)
  • 1/2 cup water, or more as needed
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (from 1 large lemon)
  • 1/2 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/8 tsp ground cayenne pepper

For The Falafel

  • Can of chickpeas, mashed with potato masher
    • Note that 1-2 Tbsp of flour can be added if needed to help the falafel hold their shape
  • 2 medium cloves garlic
  • 1/4 medium red onion, finely chopped (about 1/4 cup)
  • 1/2 small carrot, scrubbed well and finely chopped (about 1/4 cup)
  • 1 Tbsp cilantro stems, finely chopped
  • 1/3 cup loosely packed parsley leaves
  • 1.5 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • olive oil

For Serving

  • 4 pita bread rounds, each cut in half
  • 1/2 English (seedless) cucumber, thinly sliced into rounds
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 1 cup (2 oz) baby spinach
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, chopped
  • Mild harissa (store-bought; optional)

Recipe

  1. Soak the raw cashews with ~2 cups of water for 10-12 hours in a large tupperware
    1. Drain and return the nuts to the tupperware. Using a potato masher and fork, mash until no chunks remain
    2. Finely chop and mix-in the cilantro, lemon juice, salt, and cayenne pepper. Mix with a fork
      1. Add water as needed to achieve a creamy consistency
    3. (If available, this is much easier with a food processor or good blender)
  2. For the falafel
    1. In a new container, drain the chickpeas and mash with a potato masher and fork (or use a food processor if available)
    2. Add the finely chopped garlic, red onion, carrot, cilantro stems, and parsley. Add the cumin and salt
    3. Taking a Tbsp of dough and flatten into a 2” round
      1. You should now have a crumbly dough, but if two wet, add 1-2 Tbsp of flour to help the dough stick together
    4. Prep about 24 disks on a plate
  3. Cut up the vegetables for serving
  4. Heat up a thin layer of oil on the skillet under medium-high heat
    1. Once sizzling, cook 8 falafel at a time for 2-3 min per side until lightly browned
    2. We used a spatula and fork to flip, but if they stick together better, apparently chopsticks work well
    3. Add more oil for each batch until done
  5. To serve
    1. Stuff each of the pita halves with 3 falafel, cucumber slices, red onion, spinach and tomatoes
    2. Dollop with enough cilantro-cashew sauce to cover the filling, and some harissa, if using

Notes

  1. I really messed up this recipe the first time we made it. I mashed the cashews and chickpeas, so the falafel didn’t stay together and we didn’t have the cashew sauce :D
    1. The meal was saved thanks to Alex’s quick thinking of whipping together a yogurt sauce with plain yogurt, finely chopped cilantro, lemon juice, salt, and harissa
    2. This sauce could probably be improved, but was a nice way to serve the falafel if one doesn’t have cashews
