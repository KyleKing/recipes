Trinidadian Doubles

Based on Cooking with Ria (wayback), Gaz Oakley (wayback), and TriniGourmet (wayback)

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

Bara (Fried Flatbread)

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cup lukewarm water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for dough)
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Channa (Curried Chickpeas)

  • 2 (14-15 oz) cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon amchar masala (optional, or use garam masala)
  • 1-1.5 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro or culantro, chopped
  • Hot pepper sauce to taste (optional)

Condiments (Optional)

  • Cucumber chutney
  • Tamarind sauce
  • Scotch bonnet pepper sauce
  • Mango chutney

Recipe

Make the Bara

  1. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, yeast, baking powder, sugar, turmeric, and cumin
  2. Gradually add lukewarm water while mixing until a soft, slightly sticky dough forms (you may not need all the water)
  3. Knead for 5-8 minutes until smooth and elastic
  4. Rub dough with 1 tablespoon oil, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place for 1.5-2 hours until doubled in size
  5. Punch down dough and divide into 16 equal pieces, rolling each into a ball
  6. Cover balls with a damp cloth and let rest for 15-30 minutes

Make the Channa

  1. While dough is rising, heat 2 tablespoons oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat
  2. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes
  3. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant
  4. Stir in curry powder, cumin, turmeric, and amchar masala; cook for 1-2 minutes to toast spices
  5. Add chickpeas and stir to coat with spice mixture
  6. Pour in 1 cup water, add salt and black pepper
  7. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat and cook for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally and adding more water if needed to maintain a thick, gravy-like consistency
  8. Use the back of a spoon to lightly mash some chickpeas for a creamier texture
  9. Stir in fresh cilantro and hot pepper sauce if using; adjust seasoning
  10. Keep warm while frying bara

Fry the Bara

  1. Heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil in a deep pot or skillet to 350-375°F (medium-high heat)
  2. On an oiled surface or between oiled hands, flatten each dough ball into a 5-6 inch round, about 1/4 inch thick
  3. Carefully slide one round into the hot oil; it should puff up immediately
  4. Fry for 15-30 seconds per side until golden brown and puffy
  5. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels
  6. Stack fried bara to keep warm and allow them to steam slightly and soften

Assemble

  1. Place one bara on a plate, add 2-3 tablespoons of channa in the center
  2. Add desired condiments (cucumber chutney, tamarind sauce, pepper sauce)
  3. Top with a second bara, pressing gently to create a sandwich
  4. Serve immediately while warm

Notes

  • For more authentic flavor, use 1/2 lb dried chickpeas: soak overnight with 1/2 teaspoon baking soda, drain, rinse, then boil in fresh water with 1/2 teaspoon baking soda for 1 hour until tender before proceeding with step 7
  • Culantro (also called bandhaniya or shadow beni) is traditional but cilantro works well as a substitute
  • Bara should be soft, pillowy, and flexible, not dense or crispy
  • The dough can rise for up to 6 hours for even better flavor and texture
  • Makes approximately 8 doubles (16 bara)
  • This is Trinidad’s most popular street food, originating in Princes Town in 1936 when customers requested a second piece of bara to sandwich the channa filling, thus creating “doubles”
