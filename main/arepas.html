Arepas (Reina Pepiada and Con Queso)

Based on Panning the Globe, Minimalist Baker, and Immaculate Bites

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

Arepa Dough (makes 6-8 arepas)

  • 2 cups masarepa (pre-cooked white corn flour; see Notes for sourcing)
  • 2 to 2.5 cups warm water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 Tbsp neutral oil or butter (optional, for richer/more tender arepas)

Reina Pepiada Filling (chicken-avocado)

  • 2 cups shredded cooked chicken (rotisserie works well)
  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 3 Tbsp mayonnaise
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp red onion, finely minced
  • 1/2 jalapeño, seeded and minced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Con Queso Filling

  • 1.5 cups queso blanco or low-moisture mozzarella, shredded (see Notes for options)
  • Butter for spreading (optional)

Recipe

Make the Dough

  1. Combine masarepa, salt, and oil in a bowl
  2. Add warm water gradually, mixing with hands until a smooth pliable dough forms — it should not crack at the edges when pressed flat
  3. Rest 5 minutes (dough stiffens slightly; add a splash of water if too dry after resting)
  4. Divide into 6-8 equal portions and press each between palms into a disc ~3/4 inch thick and 3-4 inches wide

Cook the Arepas (Cast Iron + Oven)

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F
  2. Heat a cast iron skillet over medium heat with a thin film of oil
  3. Cook arepas 4-5 minutes per side until a golden-brown crust forms and the edges look dry
  4. Transfer the cast iron to the oven and bake 10-12 minutes until arepas sound hollow when tapped
  5. Remove and cool 2 minutes before splitting

Make the Reina Pepiada Filling

  1. Mash avocados until mostly smooth (some texture is good)
  2. Fold in mayonnaise, lime juice, cilantro, onion, and jalapeño
  3. Add shredded chicken and mix to combine. Season with salt and pepper
  4. Let rest 15-30 minutes for flavors to meld (the lime juice also keeps avocado from browning)

Make the Con Queso Filling

  1. Split each warm arepa 3/4 of the way through horizontally (like a pita pocket)
  2. Stuff generously with shredded cheese
  3. Return to the cast iron or a baking sheet and place in the oven (or under the broiler 2-3 min) until cheese is melted and starting to pull

Assemble

  1. Fill Reina Pepiada arepas with the chicken-avocado mixture and serve immediately
  2. Serve Con Queso arepas with optional butter spread inside

Notes

  • Finding masarepa in the US: Look for Harina PAN (yellow and blue bag) in the Latin/Hispanic aisle of most large grocery stores (Walmart, Target, Kroger) or any Latin market. Goya also makes a version labeled “Masarepa.” Online (Amazon) is reliable if local options fail. In Mexico, Harina PAN is available at supermercados or tiendas de abarrotes focused on Venezuelan/Colombian ingredients — masa harina (e.g., Maseca) is not a substitute; it undergoes nixtamalization which produces a distinctly earthy, tortilla-like flavor instead of neutral corn
  • Air fryer alternative: Brush arepas lightly with oil. Air fry at 370°F for 12-15 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and hollow-sounding when tapped. No cast iron needed
  • Dough consistency check: Cracks at the edges when pressing → too dry, add water 1 Tbsp at a time. Won’t hold shape → too wet, add masarepa a tablespoon at a time
  • Hollow tap test: A dull thud means the center is still raw; a lighter hollow knock means done
  • Con Queso cheese options: Queso blanco is most authentic and mild; mozzarella melts more smoothly; Oaxacan cheese (quesillo) is stringy and excellent. Avoid pre-shredded bags — the starch coating inhibits melting
  • Make ahead: Cooked plain arepas keep refrigerated for 3 days or frozen for 1 month. Reheat in a dry cast iron or air fryer at 350°F until warmed through. Make the Reina Pepiada filling up to 1 day ahead (keep avocado mashed with lime juice, covered tightly)
