Tinga Bowl

Based on https://www.halfbakedharvest.com/chicken-tinga-bowls/ (wayback) and https://www.chilipeppermadness.com/recipes/chicken-tinga/

Ingredients

Tinga Sauce

  • 1 (14.5 oz) can fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 2-3 chipotle peppers in adobo sauce, plus 2 Tbsp adobo sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Protein (choose one)

  • 1.5 lbs boneless chicken thighs, cut into strips or shredded after cooking
  • 1.5 lbs ground turkey
  • 1.5 lbs extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed

Aromatics and Vegetables

  • 1 large yellow onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil

Cilantro-Lime Brown Rice

  • 1.5 cups long-grain brown rice
  • 2.5 cups water or chicken broth
  • Juice of 2 limes
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 Tbsp butter or olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Bowl Toppings

  • 1 (15 oz) can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn (fresh, frozen, or canned)
  • 1-2 avocados, sliced
  • 1/2 cup queso fresco or cotija cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup pickled jalapeños
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges
  • Chipotle crema: 1/4 cup sour cream or Greek yogurt mixed with 1 tsp adobo sauce

Recipe

Make the Tinga Sauce

  1. In a blender, combine fire-roasted tomatoes, chipotle peppers, adobo sauce, garlic, oregano, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper
  2. Blend until smooth. Set aside

Cook the Rice

  1. Rinse brown rice until water runs clear
  2. In a pot or rice cooker, combine rice and water/broth. Bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 40-45 minutes
  3. Remove from heat, let sit covered for 10 minutes, then fluff with a fork
  4. Stir in lime juice, cilantro, butter/oil, and salt to taste

Cook the Protein and Vegetables

  1. Heat 2 Tbsp olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat
  2. Add sliced onions and bell peppers. Cook until softened, 5-6 minutes. Remove and set aside
  3. In the same pan, add protein: - For chicken: Cook chicken strips 5-6 minutes until cooked through (or shred pre-cooked chicken and add) - For ground turkey: Cook, breaking up with a spoon, until browned, 6-8 minutes - For tofu: Cook cubes 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden
  4. Pour tinga sauce over the protein. Add onions and peppers back to the pan
  5. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing flavors to meld and sauce to thicken slightly
  6. If using chicken thighs whole, shred with two forks after simmering

Warm the Black Beans and Corn

  1. In a small pot, warm black beans and corn together over medium heat, 3-4 minutes. Season with salt

Assemble Bowls

  1. Divide cilantro-lime rice among serving bowls
  2. Top with tinga (protein with sauce), black beans, and corn
  3. Add sliced avocado, crumbled queso fresco, pickled jalapeños, and fresh cilantro
  4. Drizzle with chipotle crema and serve with lime wedges

Notes

  • Traditional preparation: Often uses white rice, which cooks in 15-20 minutes and has a lighter, fluffier texture
  • Flavor/texture tradeoff with brown rice: Brown rice is chewier and nuttier. The lime juice and cilantro help keep it fresh and bright. For a lighter texture, use white rice or a 50/50 blend
  • Spice level: Start with 2 chipotle peppers and add more to taste. The adobo sauce adds flavor without too much heat
  • Sheet pan method: For easier cleanup, toss chicken/tofu, onions, and peppers with tinga sauce and roast at 425F for 20-25 minutes
  • Make ahead: Tinga sauce can be made 3-5 days ahead. Cooked tinga lasts 3-4 days refrigerated or 3 months frozen
  • Meal prep: Store components separately. Tinga and rice reheat well. Add fresh toppings when serving
  • Protein variations: Shredded rotisserie chicken works great for a quick version. Ground beef or pork also work well
