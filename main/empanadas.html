Empanadas

Based on Immigrants Table (wayback), Take Two Tapas, and Tastes Better from Scratch

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

Dough (Makes 12-14 empanadas)

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3/4 cup vegetable oil (or use 10 tablespoons butter for flakier dough)
  • 1/2 cup cold water
  • 1 egg beaten with 2 tablespoons water (for egg wash)

Traditional Beef Filling

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound ground beef (93/7 lean recommended)
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon sweet paprika
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 cup green olives, sliced
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, chopped (optional but traditional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 2 green onions, sliced

Recipe

Make the Dough

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together flour and salt
  2. Add vegetable oil and cold water, mixing with a fork until combined
  3. Use your hands to bring mixture together into a dough
  4. Knead gently on a clean surface for 3-5 minutes until soft and pliable
  5. Wrap dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes (up to 24 hours)

Make the Filling

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat
  2. Add onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes
  3. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant
  4. Add ground beef, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper
  5. Cook, breaking up meat with a spoon, until beef is no longer pink, about 8 minutes
  6. Drain excess fat thoroughly (this prevents soggy empanadas)
  7. Remove from heat and stir in olives, hard-boiled eggs if using, parsley, cilantro, and green onions
  8. Let filling cool completely before assembling (can refrigerate up to 2 days)

Assemble

  1. Divide dough into 12-14 equal portions and roll each into a ball
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball into a 5-6 inch circle, about 1/8 inch thick
  3. Place 2-3 tablespoons filling in the center of each circle
  4. Fold dough over to create a half-moon shape, pressing edges to seal
  5. Crimp edges with a fork or make a decorative rope braid (repulgue) by folding and pinching
  6. Place empanadas on parchment-lined baking sheets, spacing 1 inch apart
  7. Brush tops with egg wash for golden color

Bake

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F
  2. Bake for 20-25 minutes until golden brown
  3. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving

Air Fry (Alternative to Baking)

  1. Preheat air fryer to 370°F
  2. Lightly spray air fryer basket with cooking spray
  3. Arrange empanadas in a single layer, not touching (work in batches)
  4. Brush with egg wash
  5. Air fry for 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway through, until golden brown

Notes

  • Store-bought shortcut: Use Goya empanada discs (found in freezer section) or pie dough instead of homemade dough
  • Make-ahead: Assemble empanadas, freeze unbaked on a baking sheet, then transfer to freezer bags; bake from frozen, adding 5-10 minutes to baking time
  • Filling variations: - Chicken: Substitute shredded rotisserie chicken for beef, add 1/4 cup chicken broth - Cheese: Use 2 cups shredded mozzarella mixed with 1/2 cup ricotta and fresh herbs - Spinach & cheese: Sauté 10 oz spinach with garlic, mix with ricotta and feta - Sweet potato & black bean: Roasted sweet potato cubes with black beans, cumin, and corn
  • Serving suggestions: Serve with chimichurri sauce, salsa verde, or hot sauce for dipping
  • Argentine empanadas traditionally include cumin in the beef filling, distinguishing them from other Latin American styles
  • The dough can also be made with butter (10 tablespoons) instead of oil for a flakier, more pastry-like texture
  • Proper sealing is critical - squeeze out air before crimping and crimp twice if needed
