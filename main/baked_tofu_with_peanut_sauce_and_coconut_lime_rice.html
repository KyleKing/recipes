Baked Tofu With Peanut Sauce and Coconut-Lime Rice

Based on https://cooking.nytimes.com/recipes/… (wayback)

Personal rating: 4 / 5

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp peanut or vegetable oil, plus more for brushing the pan and drizzling
  • 2/3 cup lime juice (from about 5 limes), and zest of 1 lime
  • Kosher salt
  • 8 baby bell peppers or 1 medium bell pepper (any color will do), stemmed and thinly sliced lengthwise
  • Black pepper
  • 1 cup long-grain rice like jasmine or basmati (white or brown)
  • 1/2 cup full-fat coconut milk
  • (Optional) 2 cups mixed vegetables: bok choy, snap peas, or edamame
  • 1 cup smooth, natural peanut butter
  • 1 Tbsp red miso
  • 1 Tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 Tbsp fish sauce (optional)
  • 2 tsp chopped habanero pepper, stem and seeds removed, or 1 Tbsp sambal
  • 2 Tbsp buckwheat honey or molasses
  • 2 (14-ounce) package extra-firm tofu, drained and sliced crosswise, 1/4-inch thick
  • 3 cups peppery greens, like arugula, mizuna or baby mustard greens
  • 2 scallions, trimmed and thinly sliced

Recipe

  1. Heat the oven to 450 degrees and lightly brush a large rimmed sheet pan with oil.
  2. In a small bowl, stir 4 Tbsp lime juice with 1/2 teaspoon salt until salt dissolves. Add the sliced peppers, a few cracks of black pepper and set aside.
  3. In a small pot, combine the rice with 1 cup water (1.5 cups for brown rice) and the coconut milk. Season with salt and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook over medium-low until the rice is just tender, 12-15 minutes for white rice or 40-45 minutes for brown rice. Remove from the heat and let sit, covered, until ready to serve.
  4. In a medium bowl, whisk 4 Tbsp lime juice with the peanut butter, miso, ginger, fish sauce (if using), habanero, 2 Tbsp oil, 1 Tbsp honey and ¾ cup water. Stir until smooth and season to taste with salt.
  5. Arrange the tofu pieces in a single layer on the oiled baking sheet and season with salt. Spoon about 2 Tbsp of the peanut sauce over each, covering the top and allowing the sauce to run down and coat the sides. Drizzle the tops with some oil, and roast until glaze is set, deep brown and caramelized along the edges, 18 to 20 minutes. Add the remaining lime juice and 1 Tbsp honey to the leftover peanut sauce in the bowl to make the dressing; set aside.
  6. (Optional) While tofu roasts, quickly sauté or steam vegetables (bok choy, snap peas, edamame) until tender-crisp, 3-5 minutes
  7. Divide the greens among bowls. Add the lime zest and half the sliced scallions to the rice, then fluff with a fork. Top the greens with the rice, optional vegetables, then the tofu. Spoon the peanut dressing over everything, and garnish with the drained pickled peppers and remaining sliced scallions

Notes

  • Rice options: Brown jasmine or basmati rice can be used for higher fiber and lower glycemic impact. Increase water to 1.5 cups total (including the coconut milk measurement) and cook for 40-45 minutes
  • Flavor/texture tradeoff with brown rice: Brown rice is chewier and has a nuttier flavor than white rice. The coconut milk keeps it creamy and rich. The texture is heartier, which pairs well with the crispy tofu and peanut sauce
  • Vegetable additions: Add 2 cups of Asian vegetables (bok choy, snap peas, edamame) to increase volume, fiber, and nutrients without adding many calories. This helps balance the glycemic load
  • Meal prep: Rice and tofu can be made ahead and stored separately for 3-4 days. Reheat rice with a splash of water. Tofu can be eaten cold or gently reheated in the oven to maintain crispiness
  • Protein boost: The tofu provides about 20g protein per serving. Adding edamame increases this by another 8-9g per 1/2 cup
