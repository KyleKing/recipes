Manakish (Lebanese Za’atar Flatbread)

Based on Holy Cow Vegan (wayback), Alphafoodie (wayback), and Feel Good Foodie (wayback)

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

Flatbread Dough (Makes 6-8 flatbreads)

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water (110°F)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil

Za’atar Topping

  • 1/3 cup za’atar spice blend
  • 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil

For Serving (Lebanese Breakfast/Brunch Spread)

  • 1 cup labneh or Greek yogurt
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • 2 medium tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup Kalamata olives
  • 4 oz feta cheese, cubed
  • Fresh vegetables (bell peppers, radishes, green onions)
  • Hot tea or Arabic coffee

Recipe

Make the Dough

  1. In a small bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast; let stand 5-10 minutes until foamy
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour and salt
  3. Add yeast mixture and olive oil to flour
  4. Mix until a shaggy dough forms
  5. Knead on a lightly floured surface for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic (or use stand mixer with dough hook for 5 minutes)
  6. Place dough in an oiled bowl, turning to coat
  7. Cover with a damp towel and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour until doubled in size

Prepare Za’atar Topping

  1. In a small bowl, mix za’atar with olive oil until it forms a spreadable paste
  2. Set aside while dough rises

Shape and Top

  1. Punch down dough and divide into 6-8 equal portions
  2. Roll each portion into a ball, cover with a damp towel, and let rest for 15-20 minutes
  3. Preheat oven to 450°F with a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet inside
  4. On a lightly floured surface, roll each ball into a 6-7 inch round, about 1/4 inch thick
  5. Use your fingertips to dimple the surface of the dough (prevents excessive puffing and helps toppings adhere)
  6. Spread 1-2 tablespoons za’atar mixture over each round, leaving a 1/2 inch border

Bake

  1. Carefully transfer flatbreads to the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet (work in batches)
  2. Bake for 6-8 minutes until edges are lightly golden and bread is cooked through
  3. Remove from oven and brush edges with additional olive oil if desired

Prepare Accompaniments

  1. While flatbreads bake, arrange tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, feta, and fresh vegetables on a serving platter
  2. Place labneh in a small bowl and top with fresh mint leaves and a drizzle of olive oil
  3. Brew hot tea or Arabic coffee

Serve

  1. Serve manakish warm, allowing guests to fold or roll flatbreads with fresh vegetables and labneh
  2. Alternatively, top flatbreads with fresh vegetables and roll up like a wrap

Notes

  • Za’atar blend: Middle Eastern spice mix of dried thyme, sumac, and toasted sesame seeds; available at Middle Eastern markets, specialty stores, or make your own by mixing 2 tablespoons dried thyme, 1 tablespoon sumac, 1 tablespoon toasted sesame seeds, and 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • Quick version: Use store-bought pizza dough or naan bread, brush with za’atar oil, and bake at 400°F for 8-10 minutes
  • Cheese variation: Top with crumbled feta, halloumi, or akkawi cheese along with or instead of za’atar
  • Spinach variation: Sauté 8 oz chopped spinach with garlic, cumin, and coriander; spread on dough with cheese before baking
  • Make-ahead: Roll out dough rounds, layer between parchment paper, and refrigerate up to 3 days or freeze up to 3 months; top and bake as needed
  • Storage: Store baked manakish in airtight container in refrigerator up to 1 week; reheat in 350°F oven for 5 minutes or freeze for up to 3 months
  • Meal planning: This Lebanese breakfast spread serves 4-6 people and includes vegetables, dairy, bread, and beverages for a complete meal
  • Traditional serving: Manakish is commonly eaten for breakfast in Lebanon, either as a wrap with fresh vegetables or alongside a mezze spread
  • Manakish means “engraved” in Arabic, referring to the dimpling of the dough
