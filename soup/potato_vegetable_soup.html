Potato Vegetable Soup

Based on https://cooking.nytimes.com/recipes/1025764-potato-leek-soup (wayback)

Personal rating: 5 / 5

No image yet

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsps unsalted butter
  • 1 large yellow or white onion, finely chopped (about 2 cups)
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped (optional)
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 garlic clove, thinly sliced
  • 5 to 6 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 pounds Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes (about 5 cups)
  • 1 fresh or dried bay leaf
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Chopped chives, for garnishing
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Recipe

  1. In a large pot, melt butter over medium. Add onion (and optional carrots and celery, if using), season with salt and pepper, and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened but not browned, 8 to 10 minutes. Add garlic and stir until fragrant, 1 minute.
  2. Add broth (use 6 cups if including carrots and celery, 5 cups otherwise), potatoes and bay leaf; season with salt, and bring to a boil over medium-high heat. Cover, adjust heat to medium-low and cook at a gentle simmer until potatoes are completely tender, 15 to 20 minutes. Discard bay leaf.
  3. Working in two batches, if necessary, transfer soup to a blender and purée just until smooth. (Resist the urge to overprocess in the blender, which can result in gummy soup.) Return soup to the pot or, for an extra-silky soup, strain through a fine-mesh sieve into the pot.
  4. Stir in cream and warm over medium-low heat, stirring frequently, for about 2 minutes. Season with salt.
  5. Divide soup among bowls and garnish with chives. Serve warm, with crusty bread.

Notes

  • Loved by kids!
  • Yukon Gold potatoes have golden flesh that’s denser and sweeter than other potatoes, giving the soup a richer texture and greater depth of flavor
