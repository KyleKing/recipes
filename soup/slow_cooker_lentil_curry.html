Slow Cooker Lentil Curry

Adapted from the overly mild https://www.thekitchn.com/slow-cooker-lentil-curry-26195/… (wayback)

Personal rating: 4 / 5

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 Tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 Tbsp fresh grated ginger (or minced ginger)
  • Spice
    • 2 Tbsp garam masala (../reference/spices_garam_masala.md)
    • 2 tsp ground turmeric
    • 1 tsp cumin powder
    • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
    • 1/2 tsp paprika
    • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 cups dried brown lentils, rinsed
  • 1 (28-ounce) can fire-roasted crushed tomatoes
  • 3 cups low-sodium vegetable or chicken broth
  • (Recommended) bag of spinach, bag of peas, and drained can of chickpeas
  • (Recommended) juice of one lemon or lime, for serving
  • (Recommended) tahini or Greek yogurt, for drizzling
  • Cooked basmati rice, for serving
  • Chopped fresh cilantro leaves and tender stems, for garnish

Recipe

  1. Heat oil in the Instant Pot over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic, saute until softened
  2. Add the tomato paste and ginger, cook for 1 minute
  3. Add the ground spices and cook for an additional minute until fragrant
  4. Add the salt, lentils, tomatoes, and broth. Stir to combine
  5. Slow cook for 7.5 hours
  6. Stir in the peas and chickpeas. Start the rice
  7. In the last 5 minutes, add the spinach and let wilt
  8. Serve with citrus juice, a drizzle of tahini or Greek yogurt, and cilantro

Notes

  • Brown lentils maintain firm texture and add peppery flavor that stands up to the bold curry spices; red lentils would cook faster but break down into a mushier consistency
  • Tomato paste adds concentrated umami depth that enriches the sauce without requiring tempering
  • Fresh ginger provides brightness and aromatic punch that balances the rich spices
  • Tahini or Greek yogurt drizzled at serving provides creaminess and tang without overpowering the curry flavors
  • This simplified technique (sauté → bloom paste/ginger → bloom spices) builds complex flavor without special ingredients or steps

Comparison to Other Lentil Recipes

  • vs Red Lentil Dahl: This curry is heartier and vegetable-loaded (spinach, peas, chickpeas) vs dahl’s pure lentil focus; uses brown lentils (firm) vs red (creamy); tomato-based vs water-based; simplified technique vs traditional tempering
  • vs Lentil Soup: Both use brown lentils and similar spices, but curry has bolder Indian flavors (garam masala, ginger, tomato paste) vs soup’s lighter Mediterranean style; curry is thicker and more complex vs soup’s brothier simplicity; slow cooker (7.5 hrs) vs Instant Pot (10 min)
  • Choose this when: You want a chunky, richly-spiced curry with slow cooker convenience and complex flavor without tempering steps
