Healthy Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Cookies

Based on Healthy Oatmeal Chocolate Chip Cookies - The Big Man’s World (wayback)

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

Wet Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 2 Tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened applesauce
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Dry Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup all-purpose flour (use gluten-free if needed)
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 1/2 cups rolled oats (use gluten-free if needed)
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips

Recipe

  1. Preheat oven to 350F (180C). Line baking tray with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, combine coconut sugar, sugar, maple syrup, coconut oil, applesauce, and vanilla. Mix until fully combined.
  3. Add flour, baking soda, and salt to wet ingredients and mix well.
  4. Fold in oats and chocolate chips until just combined. Avoid overmixing to prevent spreading.
  5. Using a cookie scoop, drop dough balls onto lined trays, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
  6. Bake 15 minutes until edges begin turning golden. Do not over-bake, as cookies continue to cook as they cool down.
  7. Cool on tray for 10 minutes before transferring to wire rack.

Notes

  1. Makes 24 cookies with 96 calories each (15g carbs, 1g protein, 4g fat, 1g fiber, 8g sugar)
  2. Suggested serving size: 2-3 cookies
  3. Refrigerate dough 30 minutes for thicker cookies
  4. Use a mix of chopped chocolate and chips for even distribution
  5. Store at room temperature up to 5 days in airtight container or freeze up to 6 months
  6. Healthier alternative to traditional cookies using coconut sugar, applesauce, and coconut oil instead of butter
