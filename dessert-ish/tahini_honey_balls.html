Tahini & Honey Balls

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Nutty and lightly sweet with a sesame-forward flavor. Less sugary than most energy balls and pairs well with coffee or tea.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup tahini (well-stirred)
  • 3 Tbsp honey
  • 1 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 Tbsp sesame seeds (for rolling)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon (optional)
  • Pinch of cardamom (optional; amplifies the tahini’s nutty quality)
  • 2 Tbsp ground flaxseed or chia seeds (optional; improves binding and nutrition)
  • 2 Tbsp mini dark chocolate chips (optional; adds depth without making them overtly sweet)

Recipe

  1. Mix tahini and honey until smooth.
  2. Stir in oats, salt, and any optional add-ins until a uniform dough forms.
  3. Refrigerate 20 minutes until firm enough to roll.
  4. Roll into 1 Tbsp balls, then roll in sesame seeds to coat.
  5. Refrigerate or freeze until set.

Notes

  • Tahini brands vary in bitterness — Soom and Seed + Mill are mild and work best here; avoid any jar where oil has fully separated and gone rancid
  • If dough is too sticky, add 1-2 Tbsp oat flour or more oats; if too dry, add 1 tsp water or extra honey
  • Flavor improves after chilling overnight as the oats absorb the tahini
  • Refrigerate up to 2 weeks or freeze up to 3 months; makes ~16-18 balls
