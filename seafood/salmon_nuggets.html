Salmon Nuggets

Based on Yummy Toddler Food (wayback)

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

  • 1 pound salmon (fresh or thawed frozen, skin removed)
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 1/4 cups panko bread crumbs (plain or seasoned)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Optional Seasoning

  • 1/2 teaspoon dried parsley
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

Recipe

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or spray with cooking spray
  2. Use kitchen scissors to cut salmon into 2-inch squares (about 1-inch thick)
  3. Set up three shallow bowls: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with panko (mixed with salt and any optional seasonings)
  4. Coat each salmon piece: dip in flour (shake off excess), then egg (let excess drip), then panko (press gently to adhere). Place on prepared baking sheet
  5. Bake for 12-14 minutes until lightly golden and cooked through (internal temperature 145°F)
  6. Serve immediately with lemon wedges, ketchup, tartar sauce, or your favorite dipping sauce

Notes

  • Using kitchen shears makes cutting the salmon much easier than a knife
  • Panko creates a crispier coating than regular bread crumbs
  • Store leftovers in an airtight container up to 3 days
  • Reheat at 375°F for 4-6 minutes on a parchment-lined baking sheet (avoid microwave to maintain crispiness)
  • These freeze well after cooking. Freeze in a single layer on a baking sheet, then transfer to a freezer bag for up to 3 months
  • Pairs well with roasted potatoes, sweet potato fries, or a simple green salad
