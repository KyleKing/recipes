Dragon Noodles

Adapted from multiple sources including https://www.budgetbytes.com/pork-peanut-dragon-noodle/… (wayback), https://www.budgetbytes.com/spicy-noodle/… (wayback), and https://food.amerikanki.com/stupid-easy-recipe-for-dragon-noodles-with-crispy-tofu-1-top-rate/… (wayback)

Personal rating: 4 / 5

Ingredients

  • 16 oz bag of Lo Mein, Wavy Egg, or Pad Thai/Rice Noodles
  • some vegetables (sliced pepper, zucchini, carrots, frozen stir fry mix, etc.)
  • green onions, sliced
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup Sriracha or Chili Garlic Sauce
  • 1/2 lb ground pork or 16 oz extra-firm tofu
  • 1/4 cup unsalted peanuts (for pork version)
  • 3 eggs
  • fresh cilantro

Recipe

  1. Boil water and cook the noodles according to the directions. Drain when done and set aside
  2. Prep non-frozen vegetables and set aside. Cut the green onions into 1 inch pieces and set aside. In a small bowl, mix the sauce of brown sugar, soy sauce, and spicy sauce.
  3. For the choice of protein:
    1. For tofu, cut into 1/2-inch cubes. Heat oil in a skillet until shimmering and cook the tofu in batches until golden brown. Turn once for about 3 min per side. Store the tofu on a plate.
    2. For the pork, brown in a skillet. Remove excess grease leaving at most 1 Tbsp.
  4. For the frozen vegetables, microwave them first, drain the water, and then add to the skillet. Brown with the non-frozen vegetables
    1. For the tofu, add a little bit of butter or oil to brown the vegetables. And add the tofu last
    2. For the pork, don’t remove the pork before adding the vegetables and add peanuts
  5. Add the sauce and green onions and simmer until reduced by half
  6. Move the vegetables to the sides (or remove them) and cook the eggs
  7. Toss with freshly chopped cilantro and serve
