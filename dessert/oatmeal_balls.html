No-Bake Energy Balls

Based on https://www.blessthismessplease.com/8-no-bake-oatmeal-energy-balls/… (wayback)

Personal rating: 3 / 5

Two approaches: oat-based (quick, chewy, kid-friendly) or date-and-nut-based (richer, paleo, no added sugar). Both freeze well and work as snacks or light desserts.

Ingredients

Oat Base Ingredients

  • 1 cup rolled oats (quick or old fashioned)
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips (mini or normal)
  • 1/4 cup peanut butter (or sunflower seed butter for nut-free)
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup or honey
  • 1/4 tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 Tbsp cocoa powder (optional; intensifies chocolate flavor)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • 2 Tbsp chia seeds or ground flaxseed (optional; improves binding and nutrition)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened shredded coconut (optional; adds chew and subtle sweetness)
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon (optional)

Date & Nut Variant Ingredients (richer, no added sugar)

  • 2 cups pitted dates
  • 1/2 cup raw pecans
  • 1/2 cup raw cashews
  • 2 Tbsp cocoa powder
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 2 Tbsp almond butter
  • 1 Tbsp unsweetened almond milk
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Recipe

Oat Base

  1. Toast oats in a dry pan over medium heat 5 minutes until fragrant (optional; adds nutty depth).
  2. Combine all ingredients in a bowl and mix until uniform.
  3. Refrigerate 30 minutes to firm up (makes rolling easier).
  4. Roll into 1 Tbsp balls and freeze until set.

Date & Nut Variant

  1. Combine dates, pecans, and cashews in a food processor. Process until fine and uniform.
  2. Add cocoa powder, salt, almond butter, almond milk, and vanilla. Blend until the mixture just starts to clump and holds when squeezed.
  3. Roll 1 Tbsp portions into balls.
  4. Refrigerate or freeze until hardened.

Notes

  • Oat base vs. date variant: oat base is simpler, chewier, and kid-friendly with no equipment needed; date variant has a richer brownie-like flavor and is paleo-friendly, but requires a food processor
  • For a middle ground, swap half the chocolate chips in the oat base for chopped dates to reduce added sugar and improve binding
  • Both keep refrigerated up to 2 weeks or frozen up to 3 months; each batch yields ~20 balls
  • For adults: dip chilled balls in melted dark chocolate and re-freeze
