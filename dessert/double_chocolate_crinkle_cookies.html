Double Chocolate Crinkle Cookies

Based on Sally’s Baking Recipes (wayback)

Personal rating: 5 / 5

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (8 Tbsp; 113g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup (100g) packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1 large egg, at room temperature
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour (spooned & leveled)
  • 1/2 cup + 2 Tbsps (51g) unsweetened natural cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1 cup (180g) mini or regular size semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 3 Tbsps (35g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (120g) confectioners’ sugar, for rolling

Recipe

Preparation

  1. In a large bowl using a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar together on medium high speed until fluffy and light in color (2-3 minutes)
    1. Add the egg and vanilla extract, and then beat on high speed until combined. Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl as needed.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt together until combined. With the mixer running on low speed, slowly pour into the wet ingredients
    1. Beat on low until combined and then beat in the chocolate chips
    2. Cover dough tightly and chill in the refrigerator for at least 3 hours and up to 3 days. Chilling is mandatory for this sticky cookie dough.

Bake

  1. Remove cookie dough from the refrigerator and allow to sit at room temperature for 10 minutes (or 20 min if chilled for longer than 3 hours) until easier to scoop and roll.
  2. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C). Line large baking sheets with silicone baking mats and set aside
  3. Scoop and roll dough into balls, about 1.5 Tbsps of dough each. A medium cookie scoop is helpful here
    1. Roll each ball very lightly in granulated sugar, then generously in the confectioners’ sugar. Place 3 inches apart on the baking sheets
  4. Bake the cookies for 11-12 minutes or until the edges appear set and the centers still look soft
    1. Tip: if they aren’t really spreading by minute 9, remove the pan from the oven and lightly tap on the counter 2-3x to loosen them. Return to the oven to continue baking
  5. Cool cookies for 5 minutes on the baking sheet, and then transfer to a cooling rack to cool completely. The cookies will slightly deflate as they cool.

Notes

  1. Can I add peppermint extract? Yes, absolutely! If you want a chocolate peppermint crinkle cookie, I recommend adding 3/4 or 1 tsp of peppermint extract when you add the vanilla.
  2. Larger Batch: The recipe is easy to double in 1 mixing bowl without overwhelming your mixer. Simply double all of the ingredients. Dough chill time remains the same.
