The Great Pumpkin Pie

Based on Sally’s Baking Addiction

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

Sugared Cranberries

  • 1 cup (100g) fresh cranberries
  • 3/4 cup (180ml) water
  • 1.25 cups (250g) granulated sugar, divided (3/4 cup for syrup, 1/2 cup for coating)

Pie Crust

  • Homemade pie dough (makes 2 crusts) or store-bought
  • 1 large egg beaten with 1 Tbsp milk (egg wash)

Filling

  • One 15-ounce can (425g) pumpkin puree
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1.25 cups (250g) packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1 Tbsp (8g) cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1.5 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp ground or freshly grated nutmeg
  • 1/8 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/8 tsp fresh ground black pepper
  • 1 cup (240ml) heavy cream
  • 1/4 cup (60ml) milk

Recipe

  1. Prepare sugared cranberries (overnight recommended): Bring water and 3/4 cup sugar to simmer, dissolve sugar, cool 5 minutes, pour over cranberries, let sit 15 minutes covered. Spread on parchment to dry 1 hour, toss in remaining 1/2 cup sugar, dry uncovered at least 1 hour
  2. Prepare pie crust through step 5 per pie dough instructions or use store-bought
  3. Preheat oven to 375F (190C)
  4. Roll chilled dough into 12-inch circle on floured surface, place in 9x2-inch deep dish pie pan, tuck and crimp edges, brush with egg wash
  5. Par-bake: Line with parchment and pie weights, bake 10 minutes, remove weights, prick bottom with fork, bake 7-8 more minutes until bottom just starts browning
  6. Whisk together pumpkin, 3 eggs, and brown sugar until combined. Add cornstarch, salt, spices, pepper, heavy cream, and milk. Whisk vigorously
  7. Pour filling into warm crust (about 3/4 full), bake 55-60 minutes until center is almost set with slight wobble in middle. Cover crust edges with foil after 25 minutes
  8. Cool on wire rack completely for at least 3 hours
  9. Decorate with sugared cranberries and pie crust leaves, serve with whipped cream

Notes

  • Serves 8-10
  • Yields approximately 1 cup sugared cranberries
  • Pie crust leaves are optional decoration
  • Some leftover filling may remain; use scraps for mini pies
  • Store covered in refrigerator up to 5 days
