Homemade Chocolate Mousse

Based on https://sallysbakingaddiction.com/homemade-chocolate-mousse

Personal rating: Not yet rated

No image yet

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces semi-sweet chocolate, finely chopped
  • 2/3 cup heavy cream
  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 tsp cream of tartar
  • Pinch of salt

Recipe

  1. Place chopped chocolate in a medium heatproof bowl. Heat cream in a small saucepan over medium heat until it gently simmers (avoid rapid boiling). Pour over chocolate and let sit 2-3 minutes to soften, then stir with a metal spoon or silicone spatula until completely combined and melted. Set aside to cool slightly.
  2. In a heatproof bowl, combine egg whites, sugar, cream of tartar, and salt. Set bowl over simmering water (not touching the water) and whisk constantly about 4 minutes until sugar dissolves. Test by rubbing mixture between thumb and finger—no sugar granules should be felt, or thermometer should read 160°F (71°C).
  3. Transfer to a mixing bowl and whip on high speed with a whisk attachment until stiff glossy peaks form, approximately 7 minutes.
  4. Gently fold the chocolate ganache into the meringue using a silicone spatula until completely combined.
  5. Spoon or pipe mousse into dessert dishes. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or up to 48 hours. Cover after about an hour in the fridge.
  6. Serve topped with whipped cream, chocolate curls, raspberries, and/or fresh mint.

Notes

  • Makes 4-6 servings
  • Total time: 4 hours 45 minutes (includes 4 hours chilling)
