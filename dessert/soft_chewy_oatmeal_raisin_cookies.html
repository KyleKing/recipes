Soft and Chewy Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Based on Sally’s Baking Addiction

Personal rating: Not yet rated

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (2 sticks; 226g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • 1 cup (200g) packed light or dark brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1 Tbsp (15ml) unsulphured or dark molasses
  • 1.67 cups (209g) all-purpose flour (spooned & leveled)
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1.5 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 3 cups (255g) old-fashioned whole rolled oats
  • 1 cup (140g) raisins
  • Optional: 1/2 cup (64g) chopped toasted walnuts

Recipe

  1. Cream softened butter and both sugars together on medium speed until smooth, about 3 minutes. Add eggs and mix on high for 1 minute. Scrape bowl as needed. Add vanilla and molasses, mixing on high until combined
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk flour, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Add to wet ingredients on low speed. Beat in oats, raisins, and walnuts on low speed. Chill dough 30-60 minutes in refrigerator
  3. Preheat oven to 350F (177C). Line two baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats
  4. Roll dough into 2-Tbsp balls, spacing 2 inches apart. Bake 12-14 minutes until lightly browned on sides. Centers will appear soft. Cool on sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to wire rack to cool completely

Notes

  • Using a cookie scoop helps manage sticky dough
  • Cookies continue to set on the baking sheet during the cooling period
  • Also see the main cookie recipe: Chocolate Chip Cookies
  • Other oatmeal cookie variations: Chocolate Chip Oatmeal Cookies, Dark Chocolate Cranberry Oatmeal Cookies
